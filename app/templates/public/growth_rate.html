Growth Rate Analysis


For the growth rate module of this website, upload a single timeseries (all images at one xy position) of brightfield or phase contrast micrographs.
We find that for yeast and bigger cells, 10-20x magnification works best; for bacteria, 40-60x magnification is optimal.
PIE works best with slightly defocused images, in which cells appear as slightly brighter than the image background, with fuzzy dark boundaries.
Arrange the micrographs below in consecutive order (please note that the images will be renamed t1xy1-t#xy1 in analysis output).

maximum image size: 150 MB

Size (in pixels) of the largest empty space between cells (on the inside of a colony) to consider part of the colony. Intercell space is not typically easily identified in colonies of round cells; for these we recommend first trying the default setting (Inf). However, intercell space can often be accurately parsed in colonies of rod-shaped cells.

Whether to perform recursive cleanup step to remove pieces of background detected as part of the colony. This is helpful in certain imaging conditions but can result in parts of real colonies being missed; we recommend first trying analysis on a set of images without cleanup first. Images taken under identical imaging conditions should be consistent in whether or not they require cleanup.
Max Proportion Exposed Edge The max proportion of the edge of a detected colony ‘pie piece’ that is allowed to not touch a neighboring pie piece before being removed during cleanup step. Recommended to be set to 0.6-0.75. Only applies during cleanup steps.

Number of timepoints to include in growth rate calculation. If set to 0, use all timepoints to calculate growth rate; otherwise, report the highest growth rate for a colony that includes at least minimum_timepoint_number (enter an integer, default = 0, must be >=0)

The number of seconds elapsed between each timepoint (enter an integer, default = 3600)
